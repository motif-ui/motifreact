Welcome to **Motif UI** — a modern, robust, and flexible React component UI library built from the ground up to scale from internal projects to global products.

## Why Motif UI?

Motif UI started as an internal toolkit, but our years of experience told us it would become something much bigger. Every decision, from architecture to design, is made with global adoption in mind.

- **More than a UI library:** Motif UI is a complete design system. Everything starts from Figma as the single source of truth, ensuring design and development stay in sync.
- **Design tokens at the core:** We use design tokens for every style property, making maintenance and scaling effortless.
- **Standardized and cross-platform:** Our tokens are managed with [Style Dictionary](https://styledictionary.com), so you can export and use them across platforms, not just React.
- **Developer-first:** Simplicity is our mantra. Components are easy to use, extend, and contribute to. We welcome your ideas and improvements!
- **A true UI suite:** Motif UI goes beyond basic components. We provide unique, advanced components and features that often require third-party libraries elsewhere.
- **Minimal developer effort:** Our components are designed to do more out-of-the-box, reducing the need for boilerplate and custom wiring.
- **Effortless theming:** Customizing the look and feel is as simple as editing your design tokens and running a single script. The rest is automated.
- **Open roadmap:** We believe in building together. Your feedback shapes our direction and helps us improve for everyone.

## Key Features

- 🌐 **Design system-first**: Figma as the source of truth.
- 🎨 **Design tokens**: Consistent, scalable, and cross-platform.
- 🛠️ **Easy theming**: Change tokens, run a script, done.
- 🧩 **Rich component suite**: From essentials to unique, advanced components.
- 🤝 **Developer-friendly**: Simple APIs, easy contribution, and clear documentation.
- 🚀 **Ready for scale**: Built for both internal and global use.
- 💪 **TypeScript support**: Motif UI includes TypeScript definitions thanks to that, for TypeScript projects the types will be automatically available.
